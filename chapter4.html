1. Advantages of Sampling (CO3 – L1)

Sampling is used instead of census because:

Advantage Explanation
Saves time Data collected faster
Reduces cost Less manpower and money
Practical Census impossible for large populations
Accuracy Proper sampling gives reliable results
Feasible Works when population is infinite

2. SRSWR and SRSWOR (CO3 – L2)

(a) SRSWR – Simple Random Sampling With Replacement

  • Each unit is replaced after selection.
  • Probability of selection remains constant.
  • Same item can appear multiple times.

P(each unit) = 1 / N

(b) SRSWOR – Simple Random Sampling Without Replacement

  • No replacement after selection.
  • Each unit appears only once.
  • Probability changes after each draw.

3. Cluster Sampling vs Stratified Sampling (CO3 – L2)

Stratified Sampling Cluster Sampling
Population divided into homogeneous strata Population divided into clusters
Samples drawn from every stratum Few clusters selected randomly
More accurate Less expensive
Example: Class divided into boys & girls Example: Random classrooms selected

4. Basic Terminologies of Testing Hypothesis (CO3 – L1)

Term Meaning
Null Hypothesis (H₀) Statement to be tested
Alternative Hypothesis (H₁) Opposite of H₀
Level of Significance (α) Probability of rejecting true H₀
Test Statistic Value calculated from sample
Critical Region Region where H₀ is rejected
P-value Probability of observed result

5. Confidence Interval & Sample Size (CO3 – L2)

(a) CI for Mean (σ known)

x̄ ± Zα/2 (σ / √n)

(b) CI for Proportion

p ± Zα/2 √(pq / n)

Sample Size Formula

n = (Zα/2² σ²) / E²

Where E = margin of error.

6. Hypothesis Testing for Proportion & Mean (CO3 – L3)

(a) Z-Test for Proportion

Z = (p - P) / √(PQ / n)

(b) Z-Test for Mean (σ known)

Z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n)

(c) t-Test for Mean (σ unknown, small n)

t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n)

(d) Test for Difference of Means

Z = (x̄1 - x̄2) / √((σ1² / n1) + (σ2² / n2))
